Dunkin’ Donuts declares the start of pumpkin spice season

  • Fielding Buck
  • PUBLISHED: August 17, 2018 at 10:30 am | UPDATED: August 21, 2018 at 2:50 pm
  • Categories: California News, Eat & Drink, Latest Headlines, Lifestyle, News
Pumpkin and maple pecan coffees are returning to Dunkin’ Donuts menu. (Courtesy of Dunkin’ Donuts)

It looks like Dunkin’ Donuts will be the first fast food chain out of the gate with pumpkin-flavored coffee this year.

Dunkin’ Donuts will have its fall lineup in stores nationwide by Aug. 27, according to a news release. That date will apparently beat Starbucks, which started the whole fall-flavor craze with Pumpkin Spice Latte in 2003.

Dunkin’ Donuts’ lineup will include the return of pumpkin and maple pecan-flavored coffees. New items include an Apple Crisp doughnut with apple filling and caramel icing and a maple cream cheese spread for bagels.

Other food items include pumpkin doughnuts and muffins, Belgian Waffle Breakfast Sandwiches, and a Festive Fall Donut with red icing and chocolate and orange sprinkles.

Starbucks hasn’t made any announcements about its fall restaurant menu yet, but Fortune and other business publications have predicted it would come out on Aug. 28. Last year Starbucks launched Pumpkin Spice Latte on Sept. 5 and debuted its own Maple Pecan Latte on Sept. 22.

But Starbucks is already selling Pumpkin Spice Cookie Straws and pumpkin spice K-Cups in grocery stores.

But the coffee chains aren’t the earlier pumpkin spice purveyors out there. If you feel like spiking your coffee, Baileys Pumpkin Spice liqueur was already on liquor store shelves last month.
